A Living Legend

FOUR DECADES.
ONE VAN.

The Maruti Suzuki Omni first rolled off Indian roads in 1984 — a boxy, no-nonsense machine that quickly became the backbone of India's mobility. Ambulances, school vans, family road trips, kirana deliveries. The Omni did it all, without complaint.

The Omni E variant brought a refined MPI engine, giving it more refinement while keeping the ruggedness and spaciousness that made the Omni iconic.

Why India Chose the Omni

BUILT FOR THE
REAL INDIA

01
Unmatched Space

The Omni's boxy design is a masterclass in space efficiency. Despite its compact footprint, it fits up to 8 people comfortably — making it the go-to choice for large Indian families and businesses alike.

02
Proven Reliability

Owners routinely clock 2–3 lakh kilometers on the Omni with minimal issues. The 796cc engine is one of the most battle-tested motors in India, with spare parts available at every nook and corner.

03
Easy to Maneuver

Despite seating 8, the Omni is surprisingly agile in city traffic. Its tight turning radius and compact dimensions let it squeeze into spaces where larger MPVs would struggle.

04
Low Cost of Ownership

Annual maintenance costs between ₹2,159–₹3,165 — possibly the lowest of any van in its class. When you need cheap, reliable transportation for daily business, nothing comes close.

05
Versatile Utility

From school vans to ambulances to cargo carriers to family outings — the Omni adapts to any role with ease. It's not just a vehicle; it's a livelihood tool for millions of Indians.

06
MPI Engine Technology

The Omni E's Multi-Point Injection (MPI) engine delivers smoother power delivery and better fuel efficiency than carbureted variants — a meaningful upgrade for daily-use owners.
